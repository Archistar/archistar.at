What is Archistar?

Archistar is a research project funded by the Austrian Federal Ministry for Transport, Innovation and Technology. It is dedicated to the development of a secure distributed cloud storage architecture for trustworthy cloud usage. The Archistar framework combines methods from the domain of Byzantine fault tolerance with cryptographic techniques from secure multi-party computation and additional tools from cloud cryptography into a novel system. A software prototype is developed within Archistar, which is capable of connecting multiple cloud resources. This type of configuration is often called multi-cloud paradigm or cloud-of-clouds.

Security Features

Privacy and integrity

Privacy and integrity are guaranteed by the use of robust secret sharing when distributing information. Thus no bounded minority of nodes in the systems have enough information to recover the secret. For efficiency Archistar also supports computational secure secret sharing schemes.

Robustness

Byzantine robustness is accomplished by the use of a layer of active components in the cloud which run a PBFT protocol. Hence, beside all nodes being honest but curious, the system can also survive arbitrary malicious active attacks from insiders, as long as they less than one third of system nodes.

Availability

The idea of a distributed secure and robust system also gives increased availability. The system is immune against arbitrary failures in subsets of nodes and therefore also survive outages of single providers.

No vendor lock-in

A vendor and interface agnostic storage layer guarantees the highest flexibility for remote data storage and gives you control back over your assets. Changing providers should be easy and also result in money savings.
